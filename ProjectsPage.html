Description: The oldest of the projects made. The last project of my Sphomore year. The pomodoro timer is a method to increase focus on a certain task by concentrating all of one's effort towards it in twenty minute bursts followed by five minue rest periods. This project is what introduced me to the .time library and remains a very important mark on my journey to becoming a programmer. At the time, this was the most difficult project for me to complete, requiring logical understandings I hadnt previously possessed. Now, as shown by the other three projects, my understanding of my work has grown leaps and bounds. The Pomodoro Timer may be less complex than the other projects, but It shows progression over time.



Description: The first project of my Junor year and the first project where I implemented Object Oriented Programing. The program works to make a simulation of the card game war. Classes are used to create personalized decks for the players as well as the card identities themselves. overall, I think this was one of the most enjoyable projects to create since it had a straightforward premise which allowed most of the guesswork to be removed.



Description: One of my personal favorites which exanded my understanding of Object Oriented Programming. In this program, the computer generates three RPG characters, each with unique statistics, and runs through a series of ten battles, each with increasing difficulty. Whenever the group of characters are defeated, the fight begins again When every battle is completed with each character becoming stronger than they were previously. When each battle is won, the program prints the average amount of defeats per battle accross the entire campaign. This is one of my personal favorites because of the sheer amount of work I had to put into programing each part correctly. During this time there were still some parts of Object Orriented Programing that I still did not fully understand, but this project certainly aided my learning. This project probubly helped strengthen my understanding of programming more than any other on this list.



Description: My latest project, made at the end of my Junior year, which brings everything I learned over the course of three years and put it all together. In this project the program simulates an interactive game of Blackjack. On top of that there is a betting system implemented which starts the player with 100 credits and then ends the program when the player's bank reachs 0. While it may not be my absolute favorite on this list, it was my first true independant project where I came up with the premise and logistics all on my own. It is as of now my most complex program and shows just how far I have come over the course of four years.
